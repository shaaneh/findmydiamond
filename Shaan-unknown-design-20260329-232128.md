## Problem Statement

UK diamond buyers have no easy way to know if they're getting a fair price. The current experience is opening 10+ tabs, browsing individual jeweller websites, and trying to mentally compare prices across different certification standards, settings, and specs. There's no aggregated view with transparent pricing for the UK market.

ComparetheDiamond.com exists but positions as a retailer/etailer, not a neutral price comparison tool. The gap is a transparent, algorithmic "fair price" tool that tells buyers whether a specific diamond is overpriced, fairly priced, or a deal — without editorial bias.

## Demand Evidence

No direct demand evidence yet. The thesis rests on:
- Rare Carat proved the model works in the US (founded 2016, now a managed marketplace)
- The UK market lacks a neutral, consumer-first price comparison tool
- The founder has family in the UK jewellery business, providing domain access

**What's missing:** No conversations with actual UK diamond buyers. No validated pain point from the consumer side. The biggest risk is that UK buyers rely on jeweller relationships and don't feel the comparison pain enough to use a tool.

## Status Quo

UK diamond buyers currently:
1. Google "diamond engagement ring UK"
2. Browse individual jeweller websites (Hatton Garden shops, Queensmith, 77 Diamonds, etc.)
3. Compare manually across tabs — different specs, different certifications, different settings
4. Often give up comparing and just buy from whoever feels most trustworthy
5. No aggregated fair-price benchmark available to UK consumers

The workaround costs: hours of research, overpaying due to information asymmetry, and anxiety about whether they got a fair deal.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user (to be validated):** Someone in the UK shopping for a diamond engagement ring, likely 25-35, wants to make an informed purchase, comfortable buying online, budget-conscious but not cheap. Probably searched "how much should a 1 carat diamond cost UK" at least once.

**The assignment below is specifically about finding and talking to this person.**

**Narrowest wedge:** A single-page UK Diamond Price Check tool. Enter specs (4Cs), get a fair-price estimate and real listings ranked by price below fair price (best deals first), then by price above fair price (smallest markup first). Email capture for price drop alerts. That's it.

## Premises

1. UK diamond buyers feel enough pain comparing prices that they'd use a new tool, even though ComparetheDiamond.com already exists — **if** the tool provides something ComparetheDiamond doesn't (fair price benchmarking).
2. Family jewellery connections give supply-side advantage — access to inventory data and retailer relationships that a pure-tech competitor wouldn't have.
3. ONE user and ONE problem. Revenue model follows the demand signal.
4. Validation before building — the prototype is a demand-detection instrument, not a product.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Price Check" — Consumer-First Demand Test
Single-page tool where UK buyers enter diamond specs and get a fair-price estimate + real listings from UK jewellers ranked by value delta. Email capture for price alerts.
- **Effort:** S (engineering: ~1 week / CC: ~2-4 hours for shippable MVP; data seeding: parallel task by founder, ~1 week)
- **Risk:** Low
- **Pros:** Fastest to ship, tests consumer demand directly, email list = future customers
- **Cons:** Requires manually seeding 200-500 listings (founder task, not engineering), doesn't test retailer willingness

### Approach B: "Retailer Dashboard" — Supply-First via Family Network
Inventory management + price benchmarking tool for independent UK jewellers. Family gets the first 3-5 retailers.
- **Effort:** M (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~1 hour)
- **Risk:** Medium
- **Pros:** Solves specific retailer pain, supply ready when consumers arrive
- **Cons:** No consumer signal, longer path to proving demand

### Approach C: "Trust Layer" — Curated Marketplace Wedge
Curate 10-20 verified UK jewellers with transparent pricing + trust scores. "Wirecutter for UK diamonds."
- **Effort:** M (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~1 hour)
- **Risk:** Medium
- **Pros:** Clear differentiation, leverages family credibility
- **Cons:** Bias risk when founder's family is in the industry — raised by founder and validated as a real concern

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A (Price Check) first, then layer Approach C (Trust Layer) once trust is earned.**

The bias concern the founder raised about Approach C is legitimate. Starting with an algorithmic, transparent price comparison avoids that risk entirely. The fair-price estimate — showing whether a listing is above or below market — is what differentiates findmydiamond from ComparetheDiamond.com.

The path: Price Check (prove consumers care) → Add retailer verification/trust signals (earn credibility) → Managed marketplace (where Rare Carat ended up, and where the real revenue is).

## Technical Architecture (MVP)

- **Framework:** Next.js (App Router) on Vercel
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS
- **Data store:** Supabase (Postgres + auth, free tier)
- **Listing data model:** `{ carat: number, cut: string, color: string, clarity: string, price_gbp: number, retailer_name: string, certification_body: string, retailer_url: string | null, shape: string, created_at: timestamp }`
- **Input form fields:** Shape is locked to "Round Brilliant" for MVP (shown as a disabled dropdown so users know other shapes are coming). User inputs: Carat (number), Cut (dropdown), Colour (dropdown), Clarity (dropdown), Budget (optional, GBP). 4 required user inputs + 1 optional. Database fields use American spelling (`color`, `cut`); all user-facing copy uses British spelling (`Colour`).
- **Fair-price algorithm (MVP):** Median price per bucket (carat range + cut tier + colour + clarity). MVP restricted to round brilliant only (eliminates shape dimension, concentrates data). Carat floored to nearest 0.25 (e.g., 0.87ct → 0.75 bucket). Cut collapsed to 2 tiers: Excellent/Very Good vs. Good/Fair. If a bucket has fewer than 3 listings, widen carat range by +/-0.25 (first widen). If still fewer than 3, widen carat range by +/-0.50 (second widen). Never widen cut, colour, or clarity. Display "Estimate based on limited data" when widened. If still fewer than 3 after second widen, show "Not enough data for a fair price estimate" and display the closest listings sorted by price ascending (lowest first).
- **Bucket sparsity mitigation:** With 200 round-brilliant listings across ~8 carat ranges x 2 cut tiers x 7 colours x 8 clarities = ~896 possible buckets, most will be sparse. The widening fallback above handles this. **Seeding strategy:** Concentrate on the 10-15 most common spec combinations (0.75-1.25ct, Excellent/VG cut, G-I colour, VS1-SI1 clarity) so the most likely queries produce a real fair-price estimate. Track which queries return "not enough data" in PostHog to prioritize future seeding. Long-term: switch to regression model when dataset exceeds 1,000 listings.
- **Budget filter:** Budget filters the listing display to show only diamonds at or below the stated budget. It does not affect the fair-price estimate.
- **Email capture:** Resend (free tier). Store: `{ email, shape, carat, cut, color, clarity, target_price_gbp, created_at, gdpr_consent: boolean }`. For MVP, alerts are NOT automated — the email signup itself is the demand signal. Manual follow-up if price drops spotted. Consent checkbox required before submission.
- **Empty state:** If zero listings match the query after bucket widening, show: "No diamonds matching this spec yet. We're adding new UK listings every week." with email capture: "Be the first to know when we find one."
- **Certification scope:** MVP supports GIA and IGI certified diamonds only. Certification body is a display field, not a bucketing dimension. Disclaimer shown: "Prices may vary by certification."
- **Data seeding process:** Founder collects listings from 3-5 UK online jewellers into a CSV (columns: carat, cut, color, clarity, price_gbp, retailer_name, certification_body, retailer_url). Import via Supabase SQL import or a seed script (`bun run seed`). Required fields: all except retailer_url. Manual refresh weekly, estimated 2-3 hours/week. Trigger for automation: 10+ email signups → invest in scraping or retailer CSV upload.
- **Listing sort order:** Best deals first (price below fair price, sorted by largest discount), then above-fair listings sorted by smallest markup.
- **Listing freshness:** Static snapshot with "Last updated: [date]" shown to users. Manual refresh weekly by founder. Known limitation for MVP.
- **Analytics:** PostHog (free tier) or Plausible

## The Assignment

**This week, before you write a single line of code:**

Call three people who bought a diamond engagement ring in the UK in the last 12 months. Not friends-of-friends on the internet. Real people. Your family's jewellery business has customers. Ask to speak to three recent buyers.

Ask them: "Walk me through how you decided where to buy. What was the hardest part? Did you compare prices? How?" Then shut up and listen.

If even one of them describes the tab-switching, price-comparing, is-this-a-good-deal anxiety — you have your first user. Get their name. Ask if they'd use a tool that told them the fair price. Ask if they'd enter their email for price alerts.

If none of them felt that pain — if they all just went to the family jeweller and trusted them — you've saved yourself months of building something nobody needs.
